## Evidence tier
<!--
Pick the one that matches your situation honestly.
No tier is better than another — the right tier is the one you'll actually use.
Tiers describe how close you are to the phenomenon, not how rigorous you are.

proximal — you witness it directly. You use it yourself, you watch someone use it,
           you disable it and observe what happens. No mediation.

medial   — someone else experienced it and tells you. Feedback from a few people,
           a short interview, a session recording you watch after the fact.

distal   — the system tells you through data. Analytics, logs, metrics.
           You're reading a representation of something that happened.
